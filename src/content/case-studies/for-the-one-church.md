For The One Church (ONE Church) is an Indiana church supported across worship night graphics, Easter campaigns, banners, stickers, lanyards, and the print coordination that goes with all of it. Most of the actual print runs go directly to the print vendor, with vector files and routing approvals provided from the design side.

## The work

The Easter 2026 campaign was the biggest concentrated stretch. It included the Easter backdrop with multiple design directions (three rounds of options sent over two days), an Easter social graphic resized with the time and date inside the logo block, an Easter banner sent straight to the printer because enough cycles had been completed to skip a proof step, and Easter handouts delivered directly to the church Leadership Center. The whole campaign was about a two-week sprint.

Around the same period the following were also delivered: a Worship Night graphic refreshed with a newer photo and a date update, the Annual Organizational Meeting graphic, retractable banners in red and white (the existing black version was already in use, so a black box behind the white logo kept the new versions legible), the ONE church logo sticker vector file routed to the printer with print specs, a Mother's Day social graphic, and the ONE Kids lanyard.

The GIVE sign and Give Sticker logo (white vector per the ministry request) were coordinated through the lead and the print vendor on the same timeline.

The print coordination setup matters because it removes the middle step where a pastor has to relay messages between a designer and a print rep. When the printer has a question about quantity or surface type, the thread keeps everyone on it and the answer lands fast.
