The Crossing Church is in Sacramento, California, on Market Boulevard. The director of communications is the main contact, and the relationship came together quickly and ramped straight into Easter.

## The work

The first two months were a full sprint. The Easter sermon series was the top priority. The Party in the Park community event on March 23 needed promotional fliers, posters, and social graphics. A new Service Times banner had to replace one that had been shredded in a storm. A Family Skate Night needed colorful design across a half-page flier, an 11x17 poster, a 16:9 promo slide, and a web tile. Crossing Chair Back Cards became a recurring item once the church switched databases and redid their connection forms. The Kids Magnet project was a full rebrand of the materials first-time guest children take home.

After the Easter sprint, the cadence settled into a steady rhythm across sermon series, kids ministry, community events, and signage. Feedback came back fast and clean, usually a single note, and projects rarely went more than two rounds.

The Easter sprint is the sharpest proof point from this partnership: a full slate of holiday work moving at once, clear communication, a new service relationship that held under real-calendar pressure.
