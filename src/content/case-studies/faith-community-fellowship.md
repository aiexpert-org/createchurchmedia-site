Faith Community Fellowship in Trussville, Alabama runs on a graphic request system worth studying. Every project arrives as its own email with a clear subject line dated like 06.10.26 Graphic Request, followed by a title. Every email has the same body fields: ITEM, THEME/COLORS, text to include, sizes, deadline, and the Dropbox link for final files. The system is so consistent that any project from any month reads clearly at a glance.

## The work

The FCF design calendar is dense. In a typical recent stretch, shipped work has included: a Back to School Prayer and Worship Night graphic, Summer Fun Nights graphics with 2-up flyers, a Father's Day graphic plus a Father's Day mug and tin can logo, an At The Movies summer series (Christmas in July themed), a Family Day banner and business cards, Family Day promo graphics with an alternate Founded 250 title option, vinyl decals with scripture for a wall installation (1 Corinthians 16:13 plus a second verse, measured against the church's actual wall dimensions), Day Camp deliverables with Toy Story 5 colors and a QR code, Water Baptism and Baby Dedication updates, Graduation Sunday graphics, a Mother's Day tumbler logo, a Men's Breakfast date refresh, Women's Breakfast graphics, the RAK (Random Acts of Kindness) poster in two versions, a Father-Son Day for kids 5 to 10, and Cafe TV monitor artwork.

The vinyl decals project involved working through wall measurements in detail, confirming whether a verse would max out width-wise at a given height before the file went to the printer. That is the kind of project that does not look like design from the outside but absolutely is design.

The Family Day and Founded 250 dual title was a small request with real impact. A second proof of the same promo with one title swapped in let the team make a final naming decision with actual designs in front of them rather than picking blind.

The FCF account is what a well-organized church communications team looks like from a designer's chair. Tight briefs in, finals to Dropbox, no email drama. Both sides know how to recover quickly from the small misses that come with any real production calendar, and the system keeps the work moving.
