Eastown Church in Concord was in the middle of a rebrand effort that was consuming most of their internal design capacity. The church needed someone to hold down the smaller weekly projects so the rebrand work could keep moving on its own track.

## The work

Sermon graphics were the steady weekly throughput. The Art of Neighboring series ran across the fall, with new images delivered every week tied to that week's teaching, with titles like "People Who Move Toward" and "Fear Factor." When the rebrand effort started bumping up against deliverables, new graphics were produced to bridge the old and new brand.

The bigger project was the Christmas push. A baptism graphic was rebuilt when the original file came in missing linked photos and font files. A digital invite was designed for holiday services, sized for sharing and saving. The church's holiday calendar was updated to match the new Christmas branding. The "Make it Count" mailer went out as a direct mail postcard through a fulfillment service.

Outside the major projects came a Concord Taco Tour graphic ("El Eastown" was the title direction, with a "taco tour" subhead), a Christmas Eve service promo with a gold border treatment, and the digital invite used to push the holiday services to the congregation.
